The Satellite

A satellite is a celestial body that orbits a planet or another larger body in space. The most commonly known satellite is the Moon, which orbits Earth. However, the term also refers to artificial satellites, which are man-made objects launched into orbit for various purposes, such as communication, weather monitoring, navigation, and scientific research. Artificial satellites can be placed in different types of orbits, including geostationary, polar, and low Earth orbit, each serving specific functions. Satellites collect and transmit data, helping in areas like telecommunications, Earth observation, and space exploration. With advancements in technology, the number of satellites in orbit has significantly increased, leading to the development of satellite constellations that enhance global connectivity and data collection. The continued evolution of satellite technology plays a crucial role in modern society, impacting everything from daily communication to climate monitoring.
